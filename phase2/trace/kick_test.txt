Scenario: kick behavior and authorization checks
Date: 2026-03-11
Environment: /home/rkashko/os/project1 on os.cs.siue.edu

Goal:
Verify that the current controller can kick another connected client,
and verify that an observer is not allowed to use the kick command.

Steps:
1. Start the server:
   python3 cis_server.py ./cis.sock

2. Connect client 1.
   Expected: client 1 becomes controller.

3. Connect client 2.
   Expected: client 2 becomes observer.

4. In client 1, enter command mode and inspect participants if needed:
   Ctrl-T
   who

5. In client 1, kick client 2:
   Ctrl-T
   kick 2

6. Verify that client 2 is disconnected and returns to the local shell prompt.

7. Reconnect another observer client.

8. In the observer client, attempt to kick the controller:
   Ctrl-T
   kick 1

Observed behavior:
- The controller successfully kicked the observer.
- The server logged both "client kicked" and "client left".
- The kicked client was disconnected and returned to its own shell prompt.
- A non-controller client attempting to use kick received an authorization error.
- Controller state remained stable after kicking an observer.

Representative output seen during the test:
- [CIS] client kicked: id=2 name=user2 by id=1
- [CIS] client left: id=2 name=user2
- [CIS] only controller may kick clients

Result: PASS

Notes:
This test confirms both functionality and policy enforcement:
kick works when issued by the controller,
and kick is rejected when issued by an observer.
