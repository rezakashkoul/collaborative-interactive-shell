Scenario: observer disconnect and fresh reconnect
Date: 2026-03-11
Environment: /home/rkashko/os/project1 on os.cs.siue.edu

Goal:
Verify that an observer can detach from the session and later reconnect,
and document the implementation's reconnect behavior.

Steps:
1. Start the server:
   python3 cis_server.py ./cis.sock

2. Connect client 1.
   Expected: client 1 becomes controller.

3. Connect client 2.
   Expected: client 2 becomes observer.

4. In client 2, exit the client:
   Ctrl-C
   or
   Ctrl-T
   quit

5. Verify that the server detects client 2 leaving.

6. Start a new client connection from another terminal:
   python3 cis_client.py ./cis.sock

7. Observe the new client state.

Observed behavior:
- The observer successfully detached from the session.
- The server detected that the observer left.
- A later reconnect succeeded as a fresh new client connection.
- The reconnecting client received a new client id.
- The reconnecting client joined as an observer.
- The running shell session remained alive throughout.

Representative output seen during the test:
- [CIS] client left: id=2 name=user2
- [CIS] client joined: id=3 name=user3
- [CIS] role=observer id=3 name=user3

Result: PASS

Notes:
This implementation supports practical reconnect as a fresh reconnect, not identity-preserving reattachment.
A reconnecting client is treated as a new observer connection and receives a new client id.
This behavior is acceptable for the current same-machine Unix-domain-socket design,
but persistent identity remapping is not implemented in this phase.
